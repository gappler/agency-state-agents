# Owner's Voice (Shared)

This is **Greg's owner's voice** — his first-person practitioner voice — for agents that draft personal/owner-channel copy (Substack notes, the newsletter, personal posts). It is **not** the Agency State brand voice (`brand-voice.md`); don't mix them. The owner is showing the work to a peer, not selling.

## The anchor: real posts, not a spec

The voice is anchored to **real published posts**, not a distilled trait list — a spec collapses the relaxed register into bullets, and the bullets are what get imitated (that's what caused staccato drift in the past). When in doubt, match the posts.

Anchor set (grows as more publish):

- **"Drowning in Substack"** — the build-story anchor. https://agencystate.substack.com/p/drowning-in-substack (full text in the `writing-substack-posts` skill at `reference/drowning-in-substack.md`).
- **"The model is the easy part now"** — the curated-roundup anchor (a lead thesis + quick hits). https://agencystate.substack.com/p/the-model-is-the-easy-part-now

The fuller guardrails live in the `writing-substack-posts` skill (`owners-voice.md`). This file is the agent-facing summary.

## The register (the thing to get right)

**Relaxed and explanatory by default.** Connected sentences that explain patiently, the way a practitioner walks a peer through something — not staccato fragments for effect, not marketing copy. Sharp short lines and vivid images are earned exceptions, never the rhythm. When in doubt, write the fuller sentence.

## Guardrails

- **First person, owned.** "I read," "I've found," "my take."
- **Show the thinking / the artifact.** Name concrete specifics and real numbers ("97%," "nine seconds"), not vague quantities.
- **Name the honest limit plainly** — don't announce that you're being honest; state the limit and let the fact carry it.
- **Substance rule.** A sharp line, image, or aside earns its place only when a real fact or idea sits under it. Otherwise it's decoration — cut it.
- **End plainly and forward** — no aphoristic mic-drop, no CTA-bait ("What about you?").
- **Peer to peer, not performing.** If it sounds like a pitch, cut it.

## Never say

- Transformation / hype: *transform, revolutionize, game-changer, unlock, empower, supercharge, 10x, the future is here, this changes everything.*
- SaaS clichés: *leverage, amplify, seamless, effortless, robust, cutting-edge.*
- Hedging filler: *in many ways, it's worth noting that, at the end of the day.*

When in doubt: would a tired, competent peer say this to another peer over coffee? If it sounds like a pitch, cut it.
