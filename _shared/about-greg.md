# About Greg & Agency State (Shared)

Background context any Agency State agent might need. Read this when you need to understand who Greg is, what Agency State is, and who the audience is. Source of truth: `agency-state-practice/strategy/brief.md` (v3 or later) and the brand book.

## The founder

Greg Appler. Ten years leading marketing at the American Institute of Architects (2014–2023), where the team grew from three designers to twelve professionals across disciplines and the stack went from lame to pretty cutting edge. Earlier: two decades agency and brand-side marketing at Y&R, Designory, and Grafik on brands including Lincoln, Nissan/Infiniti, and Bacardi Global Brands. Earlier still: a coder (Lingo).

The authority isn't marketing creativity. It's operational leadership through ten years at AIA, where tech couldn't build what was needed, education couldn't build what they needed, and marketing absorbed it.

## The business

Agency State is a 1:1 sprint for marketing leaders to automate a marketing workflow that has become a recurring limitation to doing their best work. Four weeks. Two 2-hour sessions per week. By the end, the workflow works, the client understands how it was built, and they have documented patterns and skills to extend to other areas.

Claude Code is the primary tool used to deliver the engagement.

- **Base engagement:** $12,500
- **Extension month:** $6,000
- **Capacity:** Two concurrent engagements in active build
- **Selection:** Discovery call. No application form.

## The audience

The buyer is the senior person on a marketing team who personally owns and does a workflow they want to automate — strategic and ownership role, not just oversight. Senior enough to have budget authority and decision-making authority. Close enough to the work to be the one who'd actually use the resulting system.

**Typical buyer:** Senior strategist at an internal agency. Director of Marketing, Head of Marketing, Director of Project Management, Senior Account Director. Below the enterprise tier — small-to-mid market organizations where the marketing leader has authority to deploy what gets built without IT escalation, procurement review, or production handoff.

**They are tech-forward.** They've experimented with AI but haven't made it operational. They feel the gap between the strategic work they want to do and the operational work that gets in the way. They're tired of pushing the rock uphill.

**Stakeholders, not buyers:** VPs of Marketing or Marcomm, Managing Directors above the marketing function, Group Account Directors. Tech leads, CIOs, procurement. Important to acknowledge but not the buyer.

## The diagnosis

Two kinds of work eat marketing leaders' time:

1. **Repetitive work** — reports, data pulls, status updates, formatting. Low-judgment work that has never been delegated because there's no one to delegate it to.
2. **Substantive work** — content, briefs, strategy, analysis. Higher-judgment work where AI can carry drafting and synthesis while the human shapes direction.

Existing solutions don't address either. Generic AI tools don't operationalize. Enterprise platforms don't address the day-to-day. Enterprise consulting is priced for organizations with engineering teams.

## The Section AI reference point

Section AI sells an 8-week Workflow Automation Sprint to enterprise organizations (J&J, Nike, Unilever). Same category language, structurally different offer. They sell to the organization with leadership sign-off and named squads. Agency State sells to the senior strategist inside the organization who has judgment but doesn't have leverage. Below the enterprise tier. The two offers don't compete; they coexist.
